Definitions and Notations

a) Definition:
Marginal PMF is obtained by summing the joint PMF over all possible values of the other variable.

b) Notation:
p_XY(x, y): Joint PMF

p_X(x) = sum over y of p_XY(x, y)

p_Y(y) = sum over x of p_XY(x, y)


Assumptions and Preliminaries

X and Y are discrete random variables with a valid joint PMF such that total probability sums to 1.


Main Result

a) Proof:
Starting from the joint PMF p_XY(x, y), summing over all values of Y gives:

p_X(x) = sum over y of p_XY(x, y)

Similarly, summing over X gives:

p_Y(y) = sum over x of p_XY(x, y)


b) Conclusion:
Marginal distributions are obtained by eliminating the other variable through summation.


Working Examples

Example:
Given joint PMF table:

p_XY(0,0) = 1/8  
p_XY(0,1) = 1/4  
p_XY(1,0) = 3/8  
p_XY(1,1) = 1/4  


Steps:

p_X(0) = 1/8 + 1/4 = 3/8  
p_X(1) = 3/8 + 1/4 = 5/8  

p_Y(0) = 1/8 + 3/8 = 1/2  
p_Y(1) = 1/4 + 1/4 = 1/2  


Final Result:

p_X = {3/8, 5/8}  
p_Y = {1/2, 1/2}
