Properties of Rectangles

Topic 1: Properties of Rectangles

1. Definitions and Notation

Definition 1.1 (Rectangle): A quadrilateral is defined as a rectangle if it has four interior right angles ($90^\circ$). It is denoted as $\square ABCD$.

Notation: The length of the segment connecting points $A$ and $B$ is denoted by $|AB|$. The area is denoted by $\mathcal{A}$.

2. Theorems and Results

Theorem 1.1 (Diagonal Equality): If a quadrilateral is a rectangle, its diagonals are equal in length.

Mathematical Statement: If $\square ABCD$ is a rectangle, then $|AC| = |BD|$.

3. Proofs and Derivations

Proof of Theorem 1.1:

Objective: We aim to prove $|AC| = |BD|$ using triangle congruence.

Step 1: Identify two overlapping triangles, $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle BAD$.

Step 2: Observe that $|AD| = |BC|$.

Reasoning: Opposite sides of a rectangle are equal by definition.

Step 3: Observe that $\angle DAB = \angle CBA = 90^\circ$.

Reasoning: All angles in a rectangle are right angles (Definition 1.1).

Step 4: Observe that side $|AB|$ is common to both triangles.

Reasoning: Reflexive property.

Step 5: Apply the SAS (Side-Angle-Side) Congruence Criterion.

Conclusion: $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle BAD$.

Step 6: By CPCTC (Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent), the hypotenuses must be equal.

Final Result: $|AC| = |BD|$. Q.E.D.

4. Worked Examples

Example 1.1: Calculation of Diagonal Length

Problem Statement: Consider a rectangle with width $w=3$ and height $h=4$. Calculate the length of the diagonal $d$.

Solution Steps:

Identify the relationship: A rectangle contains right angles. Therefore, the diagonal divides the rectangle into two right-angled triangles. We can use the Pythagorean Theorem.

State the Formula: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$, where $a$ and $b$ are sides and $c$ is the hypotenuse (diagonal).

Substitution: Substitute $w=3$ and $h=4$ into the equation:

$$d^2 = 3^2 + 4^2$$

Calculation:

$$d^2 = 9 + 16 = 25$$

Final Step: Take the square root to find length.

$$d = \sqrt{25} = 5$$

Answer: The diagonal length is 5 units.
